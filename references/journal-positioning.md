# Journal Positioning

Use this file when selecting target journals, shaping novelty claims, writing cover letters, or revising for journal fit.

## Positioning Principle

Do not target journals by quartile alone. Match the manuscript to:

- scope and readership
- article type
- methodological rigor expected by the field
- practical, clinical, performance, or mechanistic contribution
- recent papers published by the journal
- open science, data, word count, and reporting requirements

## Journal Fit Heuristics

Broad top-tier sports medicine journals usually want:

- a clear clinical, performance, public health, or practice-changing implication
- strong design, large or strategically important sample, or synthesis-level evidence
- restrained mechanistic detail unless it changes practice
- excellent framing for a broad sports science readership

Biomechanics-specialist journals usually value:

- rigorous experimental methods
- precise mechanical variables and processing details
- clear modeling assumptions
- mechanistic explanation
- methodological transparency and reproducibility

Rehabilitation and clinical movement journals usually value:

- patient or injury relevance
- clinically interpretable outcomes
- intervention or assessment implications
- reliability, validity, or feasibility evidence

Wearable/technology journals usually value:

- validation against reference systems
- algorithm transparency
- generalizability across tasks and populations
- error metrics that matter in practice

## Cover Letter Logic

Use a short cover letter with:

1. Manuscript title and article type.
2. One sentence on the problem.
3. One sentence on the gap.
4. One sentence on the key contribution.
5. One sentence on why the journal's readership should care.

Avoid excessive praise of the journal and inflated novelty.

## Target Journal Matrix

When asked to build a target list, create columns:

- Journal
- Scope fit
- Article type fit
- Likely readership
- Required evidence level
- Methodological expectations
- Recent similar papers to verify
- Main rejection risk
- Positioning angle
- Backup journal

If journal metrics or policies are needed, verify current information from official journal, Clarivate, or institutional sources before making claims.
