# Phrasebank

Use these patterns to produce concise, cautious academic English. Adapt them to the user's actual evidence.

## Gap

- However, it remains unclear whether...
- Previous studies have primarily examined..., whereas less is known about...
- The extent to which... contributes to... has not been fully established.
- Evidence on... is inconsistent, partly because studies differ in...

## Aim

- This study aimed to determine whether...
- We examined the effects of... on...
- We compared... during... to clarify...
- We tested the hypothesis that...

## Methods

- Three-dimensional kinematics and ground reaction forces were collected during...
- Joint moments were normalized to...
- Trials were time-normalized to...
- Primary outcomes were...
- Linear mixed models were used to account for...

## Results

- Compared with..., participants demonstrated...
- The between-condition difference was...
- No clear difference was observed in...
- The effect was small/moderate/large, with confidence intervals indicating...

## Discussion

- The main finding was that...
- This result suggests that..., although the cross-sectional design precludes causal inference.
- A possible explanation is...
- These findings extend previous work by...
- The practical significance of this difference should be interpreted cautiously because...

## Limitations

- Several limitations should be considered.
- First, the laboratory task may not fully represent...
- Second, the sample was limited to..., which may reduce generalizability.
- Third, although..., we did not measure...
- Future studies should examine...

## Avoid Or Replace

- Replace "prove" with "suggest", "indicate", or "support".
- Replace "greatly improves" with a quantified effect when evidence is available.
- Replace "fills the gap" with the exact unresolved question.
- Replace "significant difference" alone with direction, magnitude, and uncertainty.
